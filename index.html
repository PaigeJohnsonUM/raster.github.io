Does Salinity affect Ocean Temperature

Hypothesis: Areas with high salt content, high salinity, will have a lower temperature than areas with lower salt content.

Salinity is measured using a scatterometer, which sends out sensory signals and then reads the backscatter to determine salt content

Chemically it requires more energy to heat water with dissolved particles than it does to heat particle free water to the same temperature.

Unfortunately it appears that there is no correlation between areas of high salinity and the temperature of those areas.

Countries form data collected from: https://github.com/vega/vega/blob/main/docs/data/world-110m.json
Ocean saline content data collected from: https://neo.gsfc.nasa.gov/view.php?datasetId=AQUARIUS_SSS_M
Ocean surface temperature data collected from: https://neo.gsfc.nasa.gov/view.php?datasetId=MYD28M&year=2015
